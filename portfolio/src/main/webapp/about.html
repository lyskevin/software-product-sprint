About Me

Computer Science

Before enrolling into NUS, I took an online computer science course (CS50's Introduction to Computer Science), which was my first proper introduction into the field. I found the lectures to be extremely interesting and the rigour of the problem sets gave me good challenges to tackle. This ignited my passion for the subject and I decided to switch majors from Information Systems to Computer Science after my first semester at NUS.

Two years later, I have developed interests in the areas of software engineering and data structures and algorithms. More recently, I have started exploring the areas of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, but I realise that I still have a long way to go, especially in terms of mathematical knowledge. I'm glad to have found my passion and am looking forward to continue learning from the amazing computer science community.

Teaching

I started teaching computer science at NUS back in 2019. The two modules which I teach are CS2030, Programming Methodology II, and CS2040, Data Structures and Algorithms. The former delves into object-oriented programming, functional programming, and medium scale software development in Java. The latter introduces students to the design and implementation of fundamental data structures and algorithms in Java.

Having come from a background with no computer science experience, I believe that everyone can learn well as long as they have the right mindset for learning. I believe in intuition-based pedagogy; I try to ensure that students understand the topics well and encourage them to ask questions whenever they feel lost. Much of computer science is built on fundamental knowledge, just like how we design programs. I also place emphasis on practice since learning the theory is often insufficient to be able to execute on it well.

I also teach mathematics at a tuition center. The tutees come from a diverse range of levels, from primary school all the way to junior college. I take a similar approach as compared to my teaching at NUS and I place more emphasis on understanding over rote learning.

Growth Investing

My interest in investing started when I was completing my National Service. I saw one of my friends checking his investment portfolio and inquired about it. Ever since then, I have been reading and investing when time permits. I invest in growth stocks because I see the most upside in them and am of the opinion that I can shoulder the risk. After all, I am not investing a significant amount of money at the moment and can afford to earn it back after graduation if I happen to lose it. I believe that managing my own money will teach me to become more financially literate and better prepare me for the working world.
